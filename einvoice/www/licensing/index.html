Licensing

The einvoice EN 16931 / XRechnung conformance engine is open source under the Apache License 2.0 — for everyone, including commercial users. Vendors who ship closed-source products and need terms the Apache-2.0 does not provide can additionally obtain a commercial license. Both paths are described below; there is nothing else to it.

Open source for everyone (Apache-2.0)

Every part of the engine — the validator package, the vendored rule corpus integration, the CI recipes, the GitHub Action and this reference site generator — is licensed under the Apache License 2.0 (the LICENSE file at the repository root). That grant is the same for a hobbyist, a consultancy and a commercial ERP vendor: you may use, modify, embed and redistribute the code, including inside closed-source products, at no cost.

The Apache-2.0 conditions are the usual ones: keep the license text and the attribution in the einvoice/NOTICE file with any redistribution, and mark files you changed. The license also contains an express patent grant and — like all open-source licenses — provides the software as is, with no warranty and no indemnity.

Commercial licenses for closed-source vendors

Some ERP, billing and e-invoicing vendors integrating the validator into closed-source products need things an open-source license cannot give them:

  • Private licensing — a negotiated bilateral license contract in place of (or alongside) the public Apache-2.0 grant, e.g. for procurement or legal-review reasons.
  • Indemnity — contractual warranties or an indemnification clause, which the Apache-2.0 explicitly disclaims.
  • Support — a named contact and a commitment on answering integration questions and tracking rule-corpus updates.

A commercial license covering any of these is available on request. Terms are agreed per vendor; no price list is published, and nothing on this page is a quote or an offer at a particular price. To be clear: you do not need a commercial license merely to embed or redistribute the engine — the Apache-2.0 already permits that, including in closed-source software, as long as you follow its conditions.

How to get in touch

Open an issue on the public repository at github.com/verifyhash/verifyhash (the Issues tab) mentioning “commercial license” and, if you can, what you are integrating the validator into. There is no contact form and no payment link on this site.
